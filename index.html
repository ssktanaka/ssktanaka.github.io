Hi, I'm Sanette

I'm a writer based in Dublin, Ireland

Nice to meet you


I am a news and tech buff, interested in data journalism, graphics and everything digital. I am a reporter by trade, and plan to transition into the online product side of media. I want to create new templates and mediums to deliver content so information can better be presented online. In essence, I want to transform how people interact with and consume media.

I am currently pursuing my master’s degree in digital media at the Dublin Institute of Technology as a George J. Mitchell Scholar. I graduated from Duke University in May 2012 with a degree in public policy and history. While there, I served as editor-in-chief of The Chronicle, Duke’s independent, student-run daily newspaper, and currently serve on its board of directors.

After a brief jaunt island-hopping in Thailand, I moved to New York City and began writing for The Wall Street Journal. For the following two years, I reported on luxury real estate, as well as other consumer lifestyle topics like food, fashion and health. I have written about the rise of meditation rooms, lucky numbers in real estate, the advent of luxury home flippers, and bidets. I’ve learned, and tried to master, skills like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby and design.

I'm a storyteller at heart. I became a journalist to tell stories that matter. By fundamentally changing the face of news, I hope to ensure that those stories continue to get told.


Feel free to contact me with freelance assignments and story ideas, or simply to say hello.
