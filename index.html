Hi, I'm Sanette

I am a writer based in Brooklyn, New York.

Nice to meet you

I’m a news and tech buff, interested in data journalism, graphics and everything digital. I am a reporter by trade, and plan to transition into the online product side of media. I want to create new templates and mediums to deliver content so information can be better presented online. At the core, I want to transform how people interact with and consume media.

This fall, I will be starting my master’s degree in digital media at the Dublin Institute of Technology as a George J. Mitchell Scholar. Until then, I plan to jet back and forth to Japan (my roots), New York (my city) and Chicago (my hometown).

I graduated from Duke University in May 2012 with a degree in public policy and history. While there, I served as editor-in-chief of The Chronicle, Duke’s independent, student-run daily newspaper, and currently serve on the board of directors.

After a brief jaunt island-hopping in Thailand, I moved to New York City and began writing for The Wall Street Journal. For the following two years, I reported on luxury real estate, as well as other consumer lifestyle topics like food, fashion and health. I have written about the rise of meditation rooms, lucky numbers in real estate, the advent of luxury home flippers and bidets. I’ve learned and tried to master HTML, CSS, Javascript, Ruby, design.

I'm a storyteller at heart. I became a journalist to tell stories that matter. By fundamentally changing the face of news, I hope ensure that those stories continue to get told.

Feel free to contact me for freelance assignments, ideas or simply to say hello.
